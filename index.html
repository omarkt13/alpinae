The problem

"What is this client's total exposure right now?" should not be a project.

When a client holds your fund plus mandates at three outside banks, answering that question means collecting four differently-formatted statements and rebuilding the picture in a spreadsheet. Slow, error-prone — and in a regulated environment, a manual error reaching a client or a regulator is a serious problem.

01

Drop the statements

Your fund's Excel printout and each custodian's statement, dragged into the client's space. Alpinae reads the account holder on every file and flags any mismatch before it touches a number.

02

Alpinae reconciles

Instrument names matched across custodians, currencies normalised, valuation dates checked. One consolidated exposure view, with the arithmetic shown.

03

Your report, your format

The consolidated view lands in the reporting format you already send — no manual assembly, nothing to re-learn. You review, you approve, you send.

By design

What Alpinae is not.

Alpinae is additive by design: it sits on top of what you already run, and asks you to change nothing.

Not a CRM No system-of-record migration, no data entry, no dependency on your CRM — or anyone else's. A lightweight client directory routes statements to the right client space; that's all.
Not a replacement for Excel Your fund printout stays an Excel printout. Your report stays in your format. Alpinae removes the reconciliation labour between the two.
Not a decision-maker Alpinae computes; you approve. Nothing reaches a client or a regulator without your sign-off, and every warning waits for your decision.
Not a client portal — until you say so Internal by default. A read-only client view exists and is switched on per client, only when you decide it should be.
